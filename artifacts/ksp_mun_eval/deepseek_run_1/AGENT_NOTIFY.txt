All 5 attempts are complete. Here's the summary:

**Attempt 1:** Reliant + 2x FL-T800 — Terrier fired in thick atmosphere at 19 km, wasted all fuel. Stage 1 too small.

**Attempt 2:** Skipper + Jumbo-64 — Ascent logic cut throttle at AP=85 km while at 42 km altitude with 25% fuel remaining. Fuel wasted.

**Attempt 3:** Skipper + Jumbo-64 — Terrier fired during coast phase (throttle got reset from 0 to 1). Escape trajectory.

**Attempt 4:** Skipper + Jumbo-64 — First stage too powerful, vessel exceeded orbital speed before staging. Negative circularization dV.

**Attempt 5:** Skipper + X200-32 — Best ascent profile (AP=147 km at burnout). But circularization dV of 910 m/s exceeded the 500 m/s sanity check, and the Terrier burned at 25% throttle during coast.

**Key learnings:**
1. The Skipper + X200-32 was the right rocket size for this mission
2. The throttle reset issue (engines firing during coast) was the most persistent problem
3. The sanity checks were too restrictive and aborted valid burns
4. Setting throttle to 0 BEFORE staging (not after) would fix the coast-phase burn issue
