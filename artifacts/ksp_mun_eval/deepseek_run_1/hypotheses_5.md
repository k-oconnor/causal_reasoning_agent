# Hypotheses — Attempt 5

## Current belief state (based on postmortems 1-4)

**The Skipper + Jumbo-64 first stage is too powerful.** It provides 4,056 m/s vacuum dV, pushing the vessel to 2,618 m/s at 62 km — well above circular orbital speed (2,310 m/s at that altitude). By the time the first stage depletes, the vessel is already on a highly elliptical orbit (AP=726 km) and needs a retrograde burn to circularize.

**New design:** Use a **Skipper + Rockomax X200-32** (smaller tank). This provides 2,976 m/s vacuum dV — enough to reach near-orbit without overshooting. The Terrier then circularizes with a prograde burn.

## Competing hypotheses

### H1: Skipper + X200-32 provides the right amount of first-stage dV
- Prediction: Skipper burns for ~77 seconds, reaching ALT > 40 km.
- Prediction: AP at Skipper burnout will be 80-150 km (not escape).
- Prediction: SPD at burnout will be 1,800-2,200 m/s (below circular speed).
- Prediction: ECC at burnout < 1.0.

### H2: The Terrier can circularize from the Skipper's burnout state
- Prediction: Circularization dV will be positive and < 500 m/s.
- Prediction: The Terrier has enough fuel to circularize.
- Prediction: PE >= 75 km after circularization.

### H3: The Spark has enough dV for TMI + MOI
- Prediction: TMI dV ~860 m/s, MOI dV ~310 m/s.
- Prediction: Spark (1,913 m/s) has enough for both.
- Prediction: At least 5% fuel remains after MOI.

### H4: Total dV budget is sufficient
- Total: 2,976 + 1,738 + 1,913 = 6,627 m/s.
- Atmospheric losses: ~1,200-1,500 m/s.
- Remaining after LKO: ~2,500-3,000 m/s — enough for TMI + MOI.

## What this experiment tests

This attempt tests whether reducing the first-stage fuel (Skipper + X200-32 instead of Jumbo-64) provides a better ascent profile where the upper stages can circularize with a prograde burn.
